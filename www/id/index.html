Daily Speed

This graph shows the speed of growth (in %) over time in Indonesia. The only parameter here is the number of confirmed cases.

Note. When the speed is positive, the number of cases grows every day. The line going down means that the speed decreases, and while there may be more cases the next day, the disease spread is slowing down. If the speed goes below zero, that means that fewer cases registered today than yesterday.

Cumulative 7-day index

Cumulative 14-day index

Mortality Level

The gray bars on this graph display the absolute number of deaths that happen in Indonesia every month during the recent five years of available data. The red bars are the absolute numbers of people died due to the COVID-19 infection.

As there is no monthly data available in Indonesia, the gray bars are the average numbers obtained via the crude death values known for this country for the recent five years of the available dataset.

Weekly Levels

This graph draws the number of deaths in Indonesia connected to the COVID-19 infection aggregated by weeks of 2020.

Crude rates

Crude mortality rate is the number of people died in a country within a year per each 1000 of population.

Here, the crude rate in Indonesia is shown for the last 50 years. The red bar against 2020 is the number of people died due to COVID-19 per each 1000 people. Thus, you can directly compare the two parameters.

Note that the vertical axis is drawn in logarithmic scale by default.

Per capita values

Here, the number of confirmations and deaths per 1000 of population in Indonesia is shown. These numbers is a better choice when comparing different countries than absolute numbers.
