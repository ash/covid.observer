Daily Speed

This graph shows the speed of growth (in %) over time in the United States of America. The only parameter here is the number of confirmed cases.

Note. When the speed is positive, the number of cases grows every day. The line going down means that the speed decreases, and while there may be more cases the next day, the disease spread is slowing down. If the speed goes below zero, that means that fewer cases registered today than yesterday.

Mortality Level

Weekly Levels

Crude rates

Crude mortality rate is the number of people died in a country within a year per each 1000 of population.

Here, the crude rate in the United States of America is shown for the last 50 years. The red bar against 2020 is the number of people died due to COVID-19 per each 1000 people. Thus, you can directly compare the two parameters.

Per capita values
