Daily Flow

New Confirmed Cases

This graph shows the number of new cases by day. The lightblue bars are the number of the new total confirmed cases appeared that day.

Daily Speed

This graph shows the speed of growth (in %) over time in Montenegro. The only parameter here is the number of confirmed cases.

Note. When the speed is positive, the number of cases grows every day. The line going down means that the speed decreases, and while there may be more cases the next day, the disease spread is slowing down. If the speed goes below zero, that means that fewer cases registered today than yesterday.

Cumulative 14-day index

This graph shows the index that some countries use for setting travel restrictions. The value is the number of new confirmed cases for the last 14 days per 100,000 of population.

Mortality Level

The gray bars on this graph display the absolute number of deaths that happen in Montenegro every month during the recent five years of available data. The red bars are the absolute numbers of people died due to the COVID-19 infection.

Weekly Levels

Crude rates

Crude mortality rate is the number of people died in a country within a year per each 1000 of population.

Here, the crude rate in Montenegro is shown for the last 50 years. The red bar against 2020 is the number of people died due to COVID-19 per each 1000 people. Thus, you can directly compare the two parameters.

Per capita values

Here, the number of confirmations and deaths per 1000 of population in Montenegro is shown. These numbers is a better choice when comparing different countries than absolute numbers.
