The height of a single bar is the total number of people suffered from Coronavirus in India and confirmed to be infected. It includes three parts: those who could or could not recover and those who are currently in the active phase of the disease.

New Confirmed Cases

This graph shows the number of new cases by day. The lightblue bars are the number of the new total confirmed cases appeared that day.

Daily Speed

This graph shows the speed of growth (in %) over time in India. The main three parameters are the number of confirmed cases, the number of recoveries and failures. The orange line is the speed of changing of the number of active cases (i.e., of those, who are still ill).

Note. When the speed is positive, the number of cases grows every day. The line going down means that the speed decreases, and while there may be more cases the next day, the disease spread is slowing down. If the speed goes below zero, that means that less cases registered today than yesterday.
