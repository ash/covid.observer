Daily Flow

The height of a single bar is the total number of people suffered from Coronavirus in the Dominican Republic and confirmed to be infected. It includes three parts: those who could or could not recover and those who are currently in the active phase of the disease.

New Confirmed Cases

This graph shows the number of new cases by day. The lightblue bars are the number of the new total confirmed cases appeared that day.

Daily Speed

This graph shows the speed of growth (in %) over time in the Dominican Republic. The only parameter here is the number of confirmed cases.

Note. When the speed is positive, the number of cases grows every day. The line going down means that the speed decreases, and while there may be more cases the next day, the disease spread is slowing down. If the speed goes below zero, that means that fewer cases registered today than yesterday.

Cumulative 14-day index

This graph shows the index that some countries use to estimate how safe travelling is. The value is the number of new confirmed cases for the last 14 days per 100,000 of population.

Mortality Level

The gray bars on this graph display the absolute number of deaths that happen in the Dominican Republic every month during the recent five years of available data. The red bars are the absolute numbers of people died due to the COVID-19 infection.

As there is no monthly data available in the Dominican Republic, the gray bars are the average numbers obtained via the crude death values known for this country for the recent five years of the available dataset.

Weekly Levels

This graph draws the number of deaths in the Dominican Republic connected to the COVID-19 infection aggregated by weeks of 2020.

Crude rates

Crude mortality rate is the number of people died in a country within a year per each 1000 of population.

Here, the crude rate in the Dominican Republic is shown for the last 50 years. The red bar against 2020 is the number of people died due to COVID-19 per each 1000 people. Thus, you can directly compare the two parameters.

Note that the vertical axis is drawn in logarithmic scale by default.

Per capita values

Here, the number of confirmations and deaths per 1000 of population in the Dominican Republic is shown. These numbers is a better choice when comparing different countries than absolute numbers.
